The dog, well-walked

Consistent daily walks — once, twice, as often as your pup needs. Easygoing dogs walk together; the handful who need one-on-one get exactly that.

A friendly face, daily

The same caregiver, every day. A little conversation, a quiet look-in on how the day's going — the kind of visit that makes a house feel less empty.

Peace of mind, delivered

For the grown kids who worry from three states away: someone reliable sees Mom every single day. That's not a dog walk. That's a weight off your chest.

Why we exist

Two lonely worlds, introduced by a good dog.

Older folks whose companion has become a little hard to walk. And people who've been counted out — the formerly homeless, the ones rebuilding — who just need someone to give them a real shot. We put them in each other's path, over the one thing everybody agrees is worth loving: a dog wagging at the door.

The whole point

It turns out the person walking your dog is smart, funny, and worth knowing — and both of you leave a little less alone than you started.

Every walker earns a real living wage, a real W-2 job, and real dignity. We hire for who someone is becoming, not the worst day they ever had.
